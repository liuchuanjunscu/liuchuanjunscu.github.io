About the Lab

About MC Lab

The Mental Conflict Research Lab (MC Lab) is part of the School of Public Administration at Sichuan University, led by Associate Professor Chuanjun Liu. Rooted in the Department of Sociology and Psychology, the lab pursues two major directions — behavioral public administration and civic education — drawing on social psychology to examine public administrative ethics, civil servant organizational behavior, and civic and public leadership development.

The lab director holds a multidisciplinary background spanning philosophy, psychology, and social psychology, with a long-standing focus on moral dilemmas and paradoxical psychological states. Recent research topics include moral dilemma decision-making methodology, school bullying, and unethical pro-organizational behavior. The lab has published 30+ papers in CSSCI and SSCI/SCI journals including Public Management Review, Journal of Business Ethics, and Public Administration, and authored Behavioral Ethics and Learning to Respect.

The lab director proposed "behavioral ethics" as a new field of study, re-examining the relationship between "goodness" (moral judgment) and "good action" (moral behavior) — arguing that inconsistency between the two is not simply "moral hypocrisy," but rather people balancing multiple conflicting ethical principles. Just as behavioral economics debunked the "rational person" hypothesis, behavioral ethics debunks the myth of the ethical "rational person."

Research Directions

Three-Level Framework

Mental conflict is nearly everywhere in social life. The lab adheres to a small-cut, big-question approach, working across three levels: Mental, Bodily, and Contextual.

M

Mental Level

MENTAL

Examines intra-individual psychological processes — cognitive performance, emotional experience, motivational change, and behavioral response. How do people react when multiple public values conflict?

B

Bodily Level

BODILY

Examines how mental conflict manifests in the body — posture, body language, bodily form, and bodily control. How do bodily artifacts (clothing, badges) shape cognition and interpersonal behavior?

C

Contextual Level

CONTEXTUAL

Examines the natural, historical, and sociocultural background factors that shape mental conflict. Uses lexical analysis, machine learning, and other methods to analyze macro-level influences.

Research Topics

Specific Topics

⚖

Moral Dilemma Decisions

Exploring psychological processes and decision mechanisms in moral dilemmas, developing the Process Dissociation Paradigm and CAN algorithm.

🏛

Unethical Pro-organizational Behavior

Studying the psychological mechanisms behind unethical acts done for organizational benefit, independently measuring ethical and organizational dimensions.

🛡

School Bullying

Researching bystander behavior, moral sensitivity, and moral disengagement in school bullying to inform campus governance.

🌱

Civic Education

Studying ethical cultivation of "respect" and civic literacy, and the development of civic and public leadership.

🤝

Prosocial Behavior

Examining how family SES shapes prosociality in children and adolescents, and the intergenerational transmission of moral decisions.

🌐

Behavioral Public Administration

Pay attention to the organizational behavior of grassroots civil servants, especially ethical behavior such as unethical pro-organizational behavior, discretionary behavior, etc. This includes multiple levels of interaction within and between grassroots civil servants, between individuals and organizations, and with the public.

Lab Culture & Approach

Research Orientation

🔬

Empirical Approach

Recognizing the dynamic nature of mental conflict, we build falsifiable theories grounded in empirical evidence, iteratively refined through practice.

🧩

Multidimensional Structure

We restore mental conflict to its natural-sociocultural context, bodily state, and agentic psychological processes.

🌐

Open Science

Every project is pre-registered on recognized platforms; data and analyses are fully open. We advocate open science and innovation through academic debate.
