Glossary

Key Concepts

Moral Dilemma

A situation where any choice violates some moral principle — not a choice between right and wrong, but between competing goods or competing wrongs.

Unethical Pro-organizational Behavior (UPB)

Unethical acts committed for the organization's benefit. The tricky part: the motive is "for the organization" but the means are unethical.

Process Dissociation Paradigm (PDP)

A method from cognitive psychology that independently measures the ethical and organizational dimensions of moral decisions.

CAN Algorithm

A lab-developed method that separately identifies Consequence sensitivity (C), norm sensitivity (N), and overall Action/inaction preference (A).

Behavioral Ethics

A new field proposed by the lab director, examining the relationship between moral judgment ("goodness") and moral behavior ("good action"). It argues that inconsistency between the two is not simply hypocrisy, but people balancing multiple conflicting ethical principles.

Goodness & Good Action

"Goodness" is what one judges to be right; "good action" is what one actually does. They often diverge. Behavioral ethics proposes this may not be "fake goodness" but a dynamic coordination of different ethical principles.

Moral Disengagement

People reinterpret behavior or deflect responsibility to feel at ease doing things they would otherwise find troubling. In school bullying, it explains why bystanders can "rationalize" their silence.
