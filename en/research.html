Overview

Three-Level Framework

Mental conflict is nearly everywhere in social life. The lab adheres to a small-cut, big-question approach, working across three levels: Mental (M), Bodily (B), and Contextual (C).

M

Mental Level

MENTAL

Examines intra-individual psychological processes — cognitive performance, emotional experience, motivational change, and behavioral response.

B

Bodily Level

BODILY

Examines how mental conflict manifests in the body — posture, body language, bodily form, and bodily control.

C

Contextual Level

CONTEXTUAL

Examines the natural, historical, and sociocultural background factors that shape mental conflict.

Research Topics

Specific Topics

Specific research topics include but are not limited to: moral dilemma decision-making, unethical pro-organizational behavior, intimate partner violence, belief violation behavior, health-environment paradox, dietary dilemmas, doctor-patient conflict, human-machine collaborative decision-making, technophobia, moral injury, mating dilemmas, and NIMBY dilemmas.

The lab currently focuses on two major directions — behavioral public administration (citizen-official interaction, policy compliance, collaborative governance, public participation) and civic education (how institutionalized schooling cultivates qualified citizens).

⚖

Moral Dilemma Decision-Making

We developed the CAN algorithm and Process Dissociation Paradigm to independently measure consequence sensitivity, norm sensitivity, and overall action/inaction preference at the individual level.

🏛

Behavioral Public Administration

Examining civil servant organizational behavior and ethical dilemmas. Recent work on creative public services and unethical behavior was published in Public Management Review.

🌱

Civic Education

The triadic theory of respect — autonomy, boundary, and equality — provides a theoretical framework and practical guidance for cultivating future citizens.

🛡

School Bullying

Daily moral sensitivity influences bystander behavior through empathy and moral disengagement. We also completed a systematic review of school bullying measurements.

🤝

Prosocial Behavior & Intergenerational Transmission

Exploring how family economic background shapes adolescent prosociality and the intergenerational transmission of moral decision-making inclinations.

🌐

Community Psychological Services

Applying mental conflict research to community governance through the "Peaceful Sleep" community psychological service project.

Research Methods

Methodology

  • Experimental psychology paradigms: classic moral dilemma paradigms (trolley problem, etc.)
  • Process Dissociation Paradigm (PDP) and CAN algorithm: individual-level decomposition of moral decisions
  • Surveys and large-sample studies across campus, community, and public organization settings
  • Caregiver-child paired surveys for studying intergenerational transmission
  • Systematic reviews and meta-analysis (PRISMA framework)
  • Data reanalysis and methodological contrasts to test robustness of existing findings

Research Approach

Three Orientations

🔬

Empirical Approach

Building falsifiable theories grounded in dynamic empirical evidence, iteratively refined through practice.

🧩

Multidimensional Structure

Restoring mental conflict to its natural-sociocultural context, bodily state, and psychological agency.

🌐

Open Science

Every project is pre-registered; data and analyses are fully open. We advocate open science.
